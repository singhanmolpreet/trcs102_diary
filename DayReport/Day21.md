# Day 21 - Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)

## Date

Day 21 of ML Training

## Objective

To understand the architecture and working of Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) and learn how neural networks are trained for prediction tasks.

## Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction to Artificial Neural Networks

* What is an ANN?
* Inspiration from biological neurons
* Applications of ANN

### 2. ANN Architecture

* Input Layer
* Hidden Layer(s)
* Output Layer
* Weights and Biases

### 3. Forward Propagation

* Flow of information through the network
* Computing outputs layer by layer

### 4. Training Neural Networks

* Loss Function
* Gradient Descent
* Backpropagation (Introduction)
* Epochs and Batch Size

### 5. Model Evaluation

* Accuracy
* Loss
* Validation Data

## Activities Performed

* Studied the architecture of an ANN.
* Understood forward propagation and backpropagation.
* Learned how neural networks update weights during training.
* Explored the workflow of training and evaluating an ANN model.

## Key Learnings

* ANNs consist of interconnected layers of neurons that learn patterns from data.
* Hidden layers enable neural networks to model complex relationships.
* Forward propagation generates predictions, while backpropagation updates weights to minimize error.
* Proper tuning of hyperparameters improves model performance.

## Summary

Day 21 focused on **Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)**, covering their architecture, training process, and evaluation. This session built upon the concepts of Deep Learning introduced on the previous day and laid the groundwork for more advanced neural network models.
